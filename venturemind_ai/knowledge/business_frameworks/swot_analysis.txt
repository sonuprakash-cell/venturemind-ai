SWOT Analysis Framework

SWOT analysis is a strategic planning framework used to evaluate a business or startup from four perspectives:
- Strengths
- Weaknesses
- Opportunities
- Threats

The purpose of SWOT analysis is to identify internal advantages and external market factors that affect business success.

1. Strengths
Strengths are internal advantages that give a startup a competitive edge.

Examples:
- Unique product features
- Strong technology
- Skilled team
- Low operational cost
- Strong brand positioning
- Innovative business model

Questions to analyze strengths:
- What makes the product different?
- What advantages does the startup have?
- What do customers value most?

2. Weaknesses
Weaknesses are internal limitations that may affect growth or performance.

Examples:
- Limited funding
- Small team
- Weak brand recognition
- Lack of market validation
- Technical limitations

Questions to analyze weaknesses:
- What areas need improvement?
- What resources are missing?
- What challenges may slow growth?

3. Opportunities
Opportunities are external factors that can help the business grow.

Examples:
- Emerging market trends
- Growing customer demand
- Technological advancements
- Untapped niche markets
- Strategic partnerships

Questions to analyze opportunities:
- What market gaps exist?
- What trends can the startup leverage?
- Which industries are growing rapidly?

4. Threats
Threats are external risks that may negatively impact the startup.

Examples:
- Strong competition
- Market saturation
- Economic downturns
- Regulatory changes
- Changing customer behavior

Questions to analyze threats:
- Who are the major competitors?
- What market risks exist?
- What external factors may affect growth?

Benefits of SWOT Analysis:
- Improves strategic planning
- Identifies growth opportunities
- Helps assess business risks
- Supports decision-making
- Improves competitive positioning

SWOT analysis helps startups understand their business position and make informed strategic decisions.
