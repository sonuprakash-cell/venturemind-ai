Business Model Canvas

The Business Model Canvas is a strategic framework used to describe, design, and analyze how a business creates, delivers, and captures value.

It consists of nine core building blocks.

1. Customer Segments
Defines the target audience and customer groups the business serves.

Examples:
- Students
- Small businesses
- Enterprises
- Freelancers

2. Value Proposition
Explains the unique value the product or service provides to customers.

Examples:
- Saves time
- Reduces cost
- Improves productivity
- Automates manual tasks

3. Channels
Describes how the business reaches and communicates with customers.

Examples:
- Website
- Social media
- Mobile apps
- Email marketing
- Partnerships

4. Customer Relationships
Defines how the company interacts with customers.

Examples:
- Self-service
- Community support
- Dedicated support
- Personalized assistance

5. Revenue Streams
Explains how the business generates revenue.

Examples:
- Subscription model
- One-time payments
- Freemium upgrades
- Advertising revenue

6. Key Resources
The important assets required to operate the business.

Examples:
- Technology infrastructure
- Development team
- Brand
- Intellectual property

7. Key Activities
The most important actions the company performs.

Examples:
- Product development
- Marketing
- Customer support
- Data analysis

8. Key Partnerships
External companies or organizations that support the business.

Examples:
- Cloud providers
- Payment gateways
- Marketing partners
- Strategic collaborators

9. Cost Structure
The major costs involved in operating the business.

Examples:
- Infrastructure costs
- Employee salaries
- Marketing expenses
- API costs

Benefits of Business Model Canvas:
- Helps visualize business strategy
- Improves planning and decision-making
- Identifies operational gaps
- Supports startup validation

The Business Model Canvas helps startups organize and communicate business strategies clearly.
